Comment: "Proven to work" is doing a lot of heavy lifting here, since the reviewer can't verify AI-generated code any better than the author who skipped it. What's changed our review culture: we now expect the PR to carry the proof, the failing-then-passing test, the trace, the before/after metric, not just a claim that it runs. Once the burden of evidence lives in the diff, AI-assisted code and hand-written code get held to the same bar.
